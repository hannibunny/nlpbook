Beer, chicken and now carbon dioxide: Why Britain’s shortages keep comingLONDON — Manufacturers of soda pop and other fizzy beverages have “only a few days” of carbon dioxide left in reserve to produce stock, Britain’s Soft Drinks Association warned Monday, adding that 340,000 jobs in the industry could be affected by the United Kingdom’s ongoing gas shortage.Carbon dioxide is used in hundreds of products to add bubbles and extend shelf life.And it’s only the latest key item that Britain is struggling to obtain amid ongoing headline-grabbing shortages, in large part due to supply chain disruptions from the coronavirus pandemic — which exacerbated problems spurred by Britain’s exit from the European Union.
