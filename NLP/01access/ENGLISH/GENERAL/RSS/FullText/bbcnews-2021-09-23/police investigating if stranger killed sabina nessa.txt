Sabina Nessa killing: Police investigating stranger attack theoryPolice are investigating whether Sabina Nessa was killed by a stranger as she walked to meet a friend at a pub.The body of the 28-year-old teacher was found by a member of the public in Cator Park, Kidbrooke, south-east London, on 18 September. Officers believe she was attacked shortly after leaving her Astell Road home at about 20:30 BST last Friday.Speaking at a press conference, Det Ch Supt Trevor Lawry said there would be an increased police presence locally.He said: "The streets are safe for women - people should be free to walk around free from fear and officers are here to make sure that can take place."Police said they were following "significant lines of inquiry" but that they were not able to confirm a motive for the attack.Officers do not believe the killing is linked to any other incident in the area.Ms Nessa was killed on a "five-minute" walk to meet a friend at The Depot bar.The journey to the pub would have taken the teacher through Cator Park, near where her body was found.Officers previously arrested a man in his 40s on suspicion of murder but he has been released under further investigation.Murdered teacher's body found hours after attackTeacher found dead near community centre namedMurder probe after woman's body found in GreenwichReport found Town Hall response to alleged sexual harassment case 'disturbing'Calls for Emma Raducanu to receive Freedom of the Borough honour in BromleyLondon fintech Prodigy Finance raises $750m from investment giantsBurglars cause 'significant damage' to Charlton HouseWhen do the clocks change in 2021 and why?Havering Hospitality Hero: Hornchurch's Tandoori Lounge racks up awardsInformation about BBC links to other news sitesFrance and US seek to mend rift over Aukus pactThe deal caused outrage in France, which lost a contract to build submarines for Australia.Covid could be more like cold by spring - expertWhat will Germans miss about Angela Merkel?
